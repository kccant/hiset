HiSET Social Studies Test

60 questions  ·  70 minutes  ·  Passing score: 8 / 20

35%

US & World History

Roughly 21 questions. Covers American history from colonialism to present, plus key world history events. Focus on causes and effects of major events — not just dates.

Civics & Government

Roughly 21 questions — tied with History as the largest section. Focus heavily on the Constitution, the three branches of government, and citizens' rights.

Test tip: Memorize the first 10 Amendments (Bill of Rights). Know that the 1st Amendment covers freedom of speech, religion, press, assembly, and petition — five freedoms in one.

Economics

Roughly 12 questions. Covers basic economic principles, supply and demand, economic systems, and the role of government in the economy. No math required — focus on concepts.

Basic economic concepts ▾
Key concepts
Scarcity & choiceOpportunity costNeeds vs wantsGoods & servicesProducers & consumersIncentives
Video resources
Khan AcademyBasic economic concepts — scarcity, opportunity cost↗ YouTubeIntro to economics — CrashCourse Economics #1↗
Test tip: Opportunity cost means what you give up to make a choice. If you spend $100 on shoes, the opportunity cost is whatever else you could have bought with that $100.

Geography

Roughly 6 questions — the smallest section, but easy points if you know the basics. Focus on map reading, world regions, and how geography affects human activity.

Reasoning skills the test also measures

Primary sources
Read excerpts from speeches, laws, and documents and identify their main argument
Charts & graphs
Interpret bar graphs, timelines, and data tables about historical or economic topics
Political cartoons
Identify the message or point of view expressed in a political cartoon
Cause & effect
Explain why historical events happened and what resulted from them
Compare & contrast
Identify similarities and differences between events, systems, or time periods
Point of view
Recognize an author's or historical figure's perspective and potential bias
