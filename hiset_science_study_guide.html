Life Science

The largest section — roughly 25 questions. Covers cells, body systems, genetics, evolution, and ecosystems. Biology is the heart of the HiSET Science test.

Physical Science

Roughly 14–15 questions. Covers chemistry basics (matter, reactions) and physics (forces, motion, energy, waves). No advanced math required — focus on concepts and reading graphs.

Earth Science

Roughly 10–11 questions. Covers geology, the water & rock cycles, weather & climate, and Earth's place in the solar system. Good area to gain quick points!

Test tip: Know the three types of plate boundaries: convergent (collide → mountains/volcanoes), divergent (pull apart → ridges), and transform (slide past → earthquakes).

Science reasoning skills the test also measures

Reading graphs & charts
Interpret data tables, bar graphs, and line graphs presented in questions
Scientific inquiry
Identify hypotheses, variables, and conclusions in experiment descriptions
Cause & effect
Determine what would happen if a variable or condition changed
Drawing conclusions
Choose the claim best supported by given data or a passage
