HiSET Language Arts · Reading

Four skills. Every passage tests them.

The HiSET Reading test doesn't separate questions by subject — it separates them by skill. Here's exactly what's tested, with free videos and a tip for each.

40%
Literary texts
60%
Informational texts
No matter which type of passage you're reading, every question on the test falls into one of four skill categories below, listed in the order the test's own framework lists them — roughly easiest to hardest.
A
Comprehension
The foundation skill, tested on every passage: can you identify what the text directly says, and how specific word choices shape its meaning and tone? These questions rarely require reading between the lines.

Test tip: Try swapping the bolded or cited word for a neutral synonym in your head. If the sentence suddenly feels flatter or different, that gap is the tone the original word was creating.
B
Inference and Interpretation
Reading between the lines. These questions ask you to support a conclusion with evidence the author implies rather than states outright — including figurative language and what a character is likely thinking or feeling.

C
Analysis
The largest and most varied skill area. These questions step back from individual details to look at the passage as a whole — its main idea, the author's purpose, structure, and the techniques used to build meaning.

D
Synthesis and Generalization
The most demanding skill, often appearing with passage pairs. These questions ask you to combine evidence — sometimes from two separate texts — to draw a conclusion, predict an outcome, or compare viewpoints.
